Startup Community

In 2013, Austin ranked the number 1 place to launch your business in a study conducted by GoodApril, a tax-planning startup. With more than 1,000 startups, Austin has been called the hottest startup scene in the USA, and is home to top incubators and accelerators like TechStars, Capital Factory, and TechRanch.

Bitcoin Community

Austin has one of the strongest Bitcoin communities. With three different weelky Bitcoin meetups whose collective membership is more than 500 cryptocurrency enthusiasts. Austin is home to Dark Wallet, Block Auth, Factom, DApps Fund, three Bitcoin ATMs, and the Texas Bitcoin Association.

Environment is Everything

Bill Aulet, MIT entrepreneurship instructor and author of Disciplined Entrepreneurship, tells us what causes MIT students to become infected with the entrepreneurial virus. The key lies in creating an environment where everyone is engaged in venture creation because all of a sudden creating a company is not impossible; in fact, everybody is doing it. The startup culture, subtle competition, and social pressures propagate the entrepreneurial virus.

Live in Bitcoin Valley

Bitcoin-friendly Texas laws are enabling innovation and growth in the Bitcoin community. The city hosted the Texas Bitcoin Conference this year and will be doing so again next year.
It is a hub for Bitcoin mining companies like Cointerra. And, there is already a strong Bitcoin investment community. Austin is home to the Decetnralized Applications Investment Fund, BitAngel founders, and BitAustin, a startup space.
